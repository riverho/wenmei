A sidecar for agent work.

Wenmei does not replace your tools. It watches what your agents do and turns important moments into actions.

Keep your workflow

Wenmei works beside VS Code, Cursor, Ghostty, Warp, Claude Code, Codex, Aider, and the other tools you already use. No migration, no replacement.

Turn agent noise into signals

Terminal output, file changes, review comments, and risky moments are filtered into the sidecar feed. Stop reconstructing a run from scrollback.

Turn signals into actions

Review a diff, reject a risky edit, steer the run, summarize what happened, resume later, or roll back. The sidecar is an action surface, not just a log.

Reviewable local work

Every edit lands as a reviewable changeset. Approve what helps, reject what does not, roll back the rest. Agent output is treated like a draft, not a deployment.

Local files, scoped rules

Works directly on local markdown folders. Paths stay inside the vault, deleted files move to trash, and meaningful actions are journaled. No import, no proprietary workspace, no cloud account.

The sidecar loop.

Watch agent work, surface important signals, act on what matters.

Keep working where you work

Use your editor, terminal, and agent setup. Point Wenmei at the local folder that matters. No upload, no sync, no conversion.

Let the sidecar watch

Wenmei observes terminal output, file changes, review events, and journal entries. It filters the run into the signals you need to see.

Act on what matters

Review a changeset, reject a risky edit, steer the agent, summarize the run, resume later, or roll back. Decisions, not scrollback.

Who is it for?

Anyone who lets an agent touch their local files and wants to stay in control.

For agent power users

Run Claude Code, Codex, Aider, or your own terminal agent. Wenmei turns messy runs into reviewable signals and actions.

For developers

Keep VS Code, Cursor, Ghostty, Warp, or your terminal stack. Wenmei adds run supervision, changeset review, and local memory.

For technical operators

Let agents update project folders, docs, specs, and scripts while Wenmei records what changed and what needs a decision.

For researchers

Collect papers, notes, and drafts in one folder. Ask the agent to synthesize, compare, or restructure. You keep the sources.

For writers

Draft in markdown, let the agent suggest edits or rewrites, and accept only the changes that match your voice.

For teams later

Use the journal and review trail to answer what AI touched, what was accepted, and what was rolled back.

Built as a sidecar, not a black box.

Local files stay local. Agent work is scoped, journaled, and reviewable. Wenmei is open source under MIT, so you can inspect the control plane instead of trusting a cloud workspace.
